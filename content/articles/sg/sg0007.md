Most Singapore freelancers start with **Excel or Google Sheets**—free and flexible. Many stall when **manual work, receipts, and habit** collide.

---

## Why the *method* matters

Your system affects:

- Whether you’ll **keep going**  
- Data accuracy  
- How painful IRAS season is  
- Hours per month on admin  

The failure mode isn’t “slow”—it’s **quitting**.

---

## Spreadsheet pros

- No subscription  
- Fully custom layouts  
- Familiar  

For **very low volume**, that can be enough.

---

## Where spreadsheets hurt

- Every line typed by hand  
- Receipts live outside the sheet  
- Little guardrails against typos  
- Charts = you build formulas + maintain them  

Common end state: **used for a week, then abandoned—or a monster file nobody trusts.**

---

## What a purpose-built tool often adds

- **Fast** mobile entry  
- **Receipt** photos tied to expenses  
- **Categories** without reinventing them weekly  
- **Reports** without formula surgery  
- **Invoices** (bonus) for cleaner income logs  

**Time saved is the product**, not “more buttons.”

---

## The real question: will you still use it in 90 days?

Excel: starts strong, often dies to friction.  
Lightweight apps: designed so **today’s lazy you** still logs the transaction.

---

## Cost vs time

If a tool saves even **30–45 minutes a week**, that’s a day or more per quarter—often worth more than the subscription.

---

## When to switch

Signals:

- Steady client revenue  
- Receipt volume rising  
- Sheet errors or duplicate rows  
- You’re procrastinating logging  

---

## Who can stay on Excel

Very few transactions, no receipt headache, happy to be manual.

Once you’re “serious,” **specialised beats heroic spreadsheet work.**

---

## Takeaway

Pick what you’ll **actually run all year**:

1. Fast capture  
2. Simple flow  
3. Habit-friendly  

That beats a “perfect” template you won’t open.
